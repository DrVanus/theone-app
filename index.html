FAQ

Common Questions

Hinge and Bumble give you unlimited matches and hope you'll eventually talk to one. The One does the opposite: you get one conversation at a time (free tier), so every match gets your full attention. We also have built-in Date Mode to plan real dates and a Graduation system that pauses your account for 30 days after a great date. Other apps want you scrolling forever. We want you to close the app.

Unlimited options lead to decision paralysis and shallow connections. By capping your active conversations, every choice becomes meaningful. Conversations also expire — 24 hours to send a first message, 72 hours of silence ends an active chat. This keeps your limited slots available for people who are actually interested.

Profiles are ranked by a 5-signal compatibility algorithm: preference alignment (30%), bio and interest similarity (25%), behavior quality (20%), compatibility score (15%), and discovery diversity (10%). Users who respond quickly, have real conversations, and don't ghost are rewarded with better matches. Users who flake drop in the rankings.

After a successful date through Date Mode, both users vote to "graduate." When both agree, you exchange real contact info. Your account then pauses for 30 days, giving you space to focus on this new connection without the noise of other matches. If things don't work out, you can skip the pause for $9.99.

Date Mode is a built-in tool for planning real dates. When both users are ready, one proposes a date. Pick from 15 shared date ideas — coffee, hiking, cooking together, live music — then set a day, time, and place. After the date, both users can vote to graduate if it went well.

Yes — The One is designed to work at every tier. Free users get the full matching algorithm, one active conversation, 5 daily likes, Date Mode, and graduation. Premium and Elite add more conversation slots, priority signals, and visibility features. The mechanic that makes The One special — the conversation limit — is most powerful on the free tier.
